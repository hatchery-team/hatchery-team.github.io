1. What?

I'm 24yo guy, looking for a small isolated team to build a strong long-lasting relationships and a common vision as a basement for our future projects.

Think of a few nerds, living together and spending most of their time developing themselfes, their relationships, and their shared philosophy.

They try together, fail together, and rise together after falling.
They share everything, talk honestly about deep feelings and try to do more so that others have less.
They spend days searching, reading, and watching; then spend nights thinking, discussing, and sharpening their vision.
They synchronize tastes and opinions and make numerous compromises, changing each other until they can understand each other without words.

And then, maybe after months or years, they start doing. If you believe in the power of one, imagine the power of a team.

You'll tell me it's utopia. I'll show you it's not.

2. Why?

2.1. Because people are worth the trouble to look for

I believe deep relationships are the most important component of our human happiness.

Think about your love partner and best friends.
How and why did you meet?
I bet it was a fluke - you just appeared in the same place doing the same thing for some time and either clicked immediately or get used to each other.

That's what I call 'naturally born' relationships.

But at the same time, we have unlimited access to other people at cost of nothing. Try to calculate how many people of your age/profession/interest there are in your city.
In the country?
In the world?..
Then try to calculate your potential speed of getting to know a new person and you'll realize that you are the bottleneck, not the people.

Given the high cost of relationships and low cost of getting to know another person, what if you try to do the intentional 'preparation work'?
What if you think over the traits of your future mate in advance?
What if you start looking right people with the right methods in the right places?

That's what I call 'artificially born' relationships and that's what I'm going to build.

2.2. Because I want to share the meaning

The feelings that bring me the highest joy ever are the shared fatigue and honor after a tough victory, the shared bliss of Evrika, and the shared pain of loss.

Without emotions being shared and intentions being completely understood, the achievements and effort lose all their meaning for me.

Why work hard, why overcome yourself, if no one can really understand what you do?

Sure, if you do something good, like cure a lot of people or invent something useful, many people will appreciate it and thank you.
But it's not enough for me. No external gratitude can replace the inner one.

This inner gratitude, the inner source of meaning is the only thing I believe, but at the same time, I'm the only author of it. Absurd.

The devastating feeling of being the only source of meaning cuts me open.

I started the Hatchery to share that onus of inevitable, endless and continuous labor of creation of meaning with the same Sisyphus-s as me.

After all, I don't want to get onto the mountain peak just to see that there is no one else there. I want to share the path with someone with whom someday we could look back and to whom I could tell: "Yes, we did it".

2.3. Because it's efficient

If you want to go fast, go alone, if you want to go far, go together.

To achieve something really valuable, you should arrange your projects like stairs - the next projects should use the previous one as the foundation. But what if you reuse not only your personal experience but the team as well?

Think about your teamwork growing together with your experience and the complexity of your projects, allowing you to implement more and more complex staff.
Everything you developed together stays with you, outlives the projects, and is reused in the new ones.
Watch and enjoy the slow, step-by-step evolution of your common speed of understanding, trust, and experience.

Knowing your mates will not change encourages you to focus long-term. You start investing time in joint growth. If someone fails, others help him to get out.

Commitment in relationships creates a solid foundation for future skyscrapers.

3. What exactly will we do?

The exact steps depend on our line-up and discussions. But generally, it would be the next four things.

3.1. Develop relationships

We will intentionally work on getting used to each other.
We will settle together.
We will distance ourselves from other relationships.
We will spend hours in deeply honest conversations about our past, present, and future.
We will talk sincerecly about our needs, fellings and fears. We will listen to each other. We will tread carefully on the slippery path of compromises.

3.2. Open the mind

We will swallow a lot of books, films, podcasts, blogs, and opinions.
We will travel.
We will build many weak ties outside and come back to hatchery to share our findings.
We will share our work with others.
We will make a lot of different projects, changing our roles.
We will try different approaches to work and live.

3.3. Build common vision

We will align on the vision of life.
We will discuss about what is important, what is not and why.
We will think carefully what we want to do.
We will argue a lot.

3.4. Develop ourselves

We will research major self-development topics and test them on ourselves.
Philosophy. Sport. Nutrition. Psychology. Task management. Art. Programming. Languages.

4. More about me

I'm weak, imperfect and fearful. I'm reflective, ambitious, selfless, lonely, and impulsive. I have average intellect, average attractiveness, average health. I spend hours thinking every day. I do sports every day. Programmer. Minimalist.

5. More about you

Here is the general "good enough" checklist I use:

  1. The Hatchery project (or whatever name) is your current life goal.
  2. I noticed three traits in you: reflexivity, ambition, and selflessness.
  3. We just clicked and I like you.

Being reflexive means that you are used to constantly thinking about what, why and how you think. It paralyzes until it is tamed, I know.
Being ambitious means you always want more than you can do and tend to make bold decisions quickly.
Being selfless means believing in your mediocrity and insignificance; minimalism and a tendency to give more than you take in teamwork. To be the guy who usually gives way and endures until he really needs something.

Here are other traits I that would appreciate:

  • Attemps to organize your knowledge and memory: diary, pkm, blog.
  • Loneliness, avoidance of crowds, noisy parties, and group training.
  • You enjoy physical activity.
  • You are a programmer / digital nomad.
